About the Data Sources

Open-Meteo (Free)

Data Sources:

  • NOAA GFS (USA)
  • DWD ICON (Germany)
  • ECMWF IFS (Europe)
  • Met Office (UK)

Strengths: Uses official government weather models, transparent data sources, hourly updates

AccuWeather (Paid)

Data Sources:

  • Same government models as above
  • Proprietary algorithms
  • Local weather station data
  • Crowdsourced observations

Strengths: Additional local adjustments, MinuteCast for precipitation, established brand

National Weather Service (Baseline)

Data Sources:

  • Direct NOAA/NWS data
  • Official US government forecasts
  • No commercial processing

Strengths: Most authoritative for US locations, free, no bias

Note: This would be the "ground truth" for US locations

Typical Forecast Accuracy

Based on independent studies:

  • 24-hour forecasts: 85-95% accurate for temperature (±3°F), 70-80% for precipitation
  • 3-day forecasts: 75-85% accurate for temperature, 60-70% for precipitation
  • 7-day forecasts: 60-70% accurate for temperature, 50% for precipitation

Key insight: Most commercial weather services (AccuWeather, Weather.com, etc.) use the same underlying government models. Differences come from:

  • How they interpret and blend multiple models
  • Local adjustments from weather stations
  • Proprietary algorithms for specific predictions (like precipitation timing)
